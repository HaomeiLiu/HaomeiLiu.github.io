An Energy-Efficient 4 Kb 10T SRAM Array with In-Memory Computing

Posted on 2022-05-05

Summary

In this project for USC EE577a (VLSI System Design), we designed a 4KB 10T SRAM with in-memory computing capability and low power technique. Our design supports two modes of read: normal read, which functions as a standard SRAM, and in-memory read, which returns the XOR result of the two given address using in-memory computation. The overall energy consumption during both normal and in-memory operations is reduced by applying a lowered Vdd on the SRAM array.

Tool: Cadence Virtuoso

Key Design Features

  • 10T SRAM cell with seperate read and write BL/WL

  • Asymmetric current mode sense amplifier

  • Seperate voltage supply for decoder and SRAM array: Vdd for the decoder is maintained at 1v as reducing it would cause significant delay. A seperate Vdd lowered to 0.85v is applied to the rest of the circuit to reduce power.

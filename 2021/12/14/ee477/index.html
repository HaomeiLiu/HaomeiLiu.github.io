VLSI Project - 6-bit ALU

Posted on 2021-12-14 Edited on 2021-12-26

Summary

In this project for USC EE477 (MOS VLSI Design), we designed a 6-bit ALU using Cadence Virtuoso. Our group of 2 is among the TOP 6 teams of a 60-student class. Peformance ranking is based on circuit_total_area * clock_period.

Components in this project include:

  • a 12-bit adder
  • a 6-bit multiplier
  • a 6-bit left shifter
  • a 12-bit D Flip-flop
  • a 4-to-1 Mux

The worst case delay happens in a MAC operation, during which inputs are first multiplied, then added with the stored value in the register.

Design Choice

We decided to use Dadda Tree Multiplier and Brent-Kung Adder architecture. Sizing was given extra consideration on time critical paths.

What can be improved?

  • Architecture: we chose the architecture because theoretically less transistors are used. However the asymetrical nature of these designs make layout harder, and does not necessarily cut the area of layout.
  • Ctitical Path Opmization
  • Pipeline the ALU: initially we considered to put a pipeline register on the MAC path to reduce the worst case delay. It was not implemented due to time constraints, but it has the potential to reduce clock period significantly without increasing too much of the area.

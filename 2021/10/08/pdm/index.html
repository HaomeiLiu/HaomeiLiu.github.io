USC Racing - Power Distribution Module

Posted on 2021-10-08 Edited on 2021-12-26

Summary

Power distribution has always been a problem on our past cars. The power distribution module is electrical team’s attempt to integrate messy wires and components into one elegant piece of PCB.

Problem Statement

Power distribution is largely an analog electrical system in FSAE electrical systems. Introducing digital control to the system will allow efficient use of the available power and intelligent routing of that power to the subsystems that require it. Furthermore, it is an opportunity to consolidate multiple systems on the electronics front plate into one place to fulfill the goal of simplifying the cable harness on SCR21.

Use the board-space for a microcontroller and current sensors to monitor power-flow, for a 12V-5V converter for 5V power-rail on the vehicle, and an SD-card for data-logging via the vehicle’s Controller Area Network. This eliminates the need for an external 5V converter and data loggers as used in previous years. This replaces nearly 2lbs of components with a couple of grams at most and simplifies the electronics harness.

—PDM Report

Design Procedure

  • breadboard prototype (in this case, we had these parts tested in previous years’ cars, just in separate components), software ready in use
  • PCB drawing (Autodesk fusion turned out to be a bad choice)
  • Manufacture
  • Testing (by parts: high to low voltage)

CAN

CAN transceiver - MCP2561 by Microchip, low standby current consumption

Special consideration in PCB design: decoupling caps, CANH and CANL uses differential transmission, so close to each other, self-oscillation filter resistor between CANH and CANL.

Buck

Why not LDO? (low dropout)

  • Difference between input and output voltage is too big (7v)
  • High power requirement (high output current)
  • Supply to digital - not very sensitive to noise

Improvements for SC22 Iteration

  • Seperate analog and digital: keep analog in PDM, move digital parts to dash
  • Some traces and parts are revised for better eletrical characteristics or easier soldering.
